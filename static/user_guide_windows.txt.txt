A. Export logs using Event Viewer (GUI)
1. Press Windows key, type "Event Viewer" and open it.
2. In left panel expand "Windows Logs". Choose "System", "Security" or "Application".
3. Click the log you want (e.g., "System").
4. In right pane under "Actions", click "Save All Events As...".
5. Choose a filename and save as .evtx (example: C:\Users\You\Desktop\system.evtx).

B. Convert EVTX to text for Logix (PowerShell)
1. Open PowerShell (Run as user).
2. Run:
   Get-WinEvent -Path "C:\path\to\system.evtx" | Format-List | Out-File -FilePath "C:\path\to\system_export.txt" -Encoding utf8

C. Quick PowerShell export (last 24 hours)
   Get-WinEvent -FilterHashtable @{LogName='System'; StartTime=(Get-Date).AddDays(-1)} | Format-List | Out-File -FilePath "C:\path\to\system_lastday.txt" -Encoding utf8

D. Precautions (must read)
- If you suspect an active compromise, disconnect the device from the network before exporting logs if possible.
- Avoid rebooting the machine unless instructed by incident responders.
- Export only logs (text/evtx). Don't upload entire disk images or personal files.
- Share reports only with authorized security personnel.
